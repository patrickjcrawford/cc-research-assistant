# Talk Format Constraints

| Format | Duration | Slides | Audience |
|--------|----------|--------|----------|
| Job Market | 45-50 min | 35-45 | Faculty hiring committees |
| Seminar | 60-75 min | 40-55 | Department seminar |
| Short | 15-20 min | 12-18 | Conference session |
| Lightning | 5-7 min | 5-8 | Poster session / speed round |

## Per-Format Rules

### Job Market Talk
- First 5 minutes: question + contribution (before any model/data)
- Show identification strategy by slide 10
- Main result by slide 20
- Leave 10 minutes for questions
- Backup slides for anticipated questions (3-5 minimum)
- This is the most important format. Allocate time for iteration.

### Seminar
- More methodological detail allowed
- Can show robustness in main deck
- Discussion section expected
- Audience may interrupt with questions -- build in natural pause points
- Main result by the halfway mark

### Short (Conference)
- One main result only
- Skip literature review
- Minimal robustness (backup slides)
- End with "big picture" not "future work"
- No time for audience to absorb complexity -- simplify aggressively

### Lightning
- One idea, one figure, one takeaway
- No equations
- No tables (figures only)
- The goal is to make the audience want to read the paper
- Practice to the second -- 5 minutes goes fast
